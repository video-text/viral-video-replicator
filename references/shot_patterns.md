# Viral ecommerce shot patterns

Use these patterns to classify and recreate reference videos.

## 1. chaos demand hook

Example mechanics: crowd rushes product, staff controls order, “only one per person”.

Use when the product can plausibly trigger urgency or novelty.

Structure:
- 0:00-0:03 crowd/action chaos around product
- 0:03-0:06 authority figure or narrator creates scarcity
- 0:06-0:12 product benefits and close-ups
- 0:12-0:15 CTA

Prompt cues:
- crowded warehouse, store aisle, pop-up booth, packing table
- handheld camera shake, fast push-in, urgent motion
- product boxes or product hero object clearly visible
- avoid real brand signage and fake public panic

## 2. problem interruption hook

Example mechanics: a person is struggling, another person interrupts with the product.

Structure:
- problem in first second
- product enters frame immediately
- quick demo
- relief reaction
- CTA

Prompt cues:
- close-up of discomfort or inconvenience
- product enters from side of frame
- natural ugc lighting
- clear before/after feeling, not medical cure

## 3. comparison hook

Example mechanics: old method vs new product.

Structure:
- old way looks messy, slow, or frustrating
- new product appears
- side-by-side or rapid transition
- benefit stack
- CTA

Prompt cues:
- split-screen or quick cut
- text overlays: “old way” / “new way”
- clean product demonstration

## 4. authority demo hook

Example mechanics: expert, worker, driver, stylist, parent, or athlete explains the product.

Structure:
- credible user archetype appears in first shot
- one direct claim or problem statement
- close-up demonstration
- CTA

Prompt cues:
- occupational costume only when relevant and generic
- no impersonation of real experts or real institutions
- use “real user review” style for authenticity

## 5. unboxing discovery hook

Example mechanics: box opens, product revealed, immediate first-use reaction.

Structure:
- package enters frame
- fast unboxing
- product close-up
- use case demonstration
- CTA

Prompt cues:
- tabletop, hands, clean lighting
- crisp product reveal
- macro details and tactile sounds if relevant

## 6. lifestyle transformation hook

Example mechanics: messy/stressed moment turns into clean/relaxed routine.

Structure:
- emotional problem
- product introduced
- relaxed demonstration
- visual payoff
- CTA

Prompt cues:
- home bathroom, bedroom, gym bag, desk, car, kitchen
- warm natural light
- product in hand and in use
