# Safe recreation boundaries

## Allowed transformation

It is acceptable to recreate high-level ad mechanics:
- hook type
- pacing and shot rhythm
- product role
- generic scene layout
- selling sequence
- camera distance and motion style
- conversion structure

## Avoid copying

Do not reproduce:
- original watermarks or platform marks
- real creator name, likeness, voice, or catchphrases
- exact music or sound design from the reference
- exact captions if they are distinctive
- protected characters, celebrities, influencers, or brand mascots
- recognizable store signage, logos, packaging, or trade dress from third parties
- footage that appears to imply official endorsement or real public events

## Claims guidance

For health, beauty, massage, recovery, and wellness products:
- avoid guaranteed outcomes
- avoid medical cure claims
- avoid before/after claims that imply clinical proof
- prefer safe language: “helps relax”, “designed for daily care”, “supports comfort”, “feels soothing”, “easy to use at home”

For electronics:
- do not invent specifications such as battery life, decibel ratings, waterproof levels, or certifications
- if specs are provided by the user, include them clearly
- if specs are unknown, use visible or generic benefits only

For scarcity and social proof:
- avoid fake exact numbers unless supplied
- acceptable: “limited batch”, “selling fast”, “people are noticing it”
- avoid deceptive claims like “everyone is buying this” unless framed as dramatized ad concept

## Safer wording for replication

Use:
- “保留爆款结构”
- “复刻节奏和镜头逻辑”
- “改写成原创素材”
- “使用新的产品、人物、场景和字幕”

Avoid:
- “一比一搬运”
- “原视频换脸”
- “保留原作者声音/音乐/水印”
- “完全一样的字幕和人物”
